Invitations to Linguistics

Features of human language

1. Arbitrariness

The relationship between speech sounds [and spellings] and the meanings they represent in the languages of the world is, for the most part, an arbitrary one.

2. Duality

3. Creativity (productivity)

The grammatical rules and words of a language are finite, but the sentences are infinite.

4. Displacement

Language can be used to refer to things real or imagined, past, present, or future.

That is, what can be spoken is not limited by time and space (cf. a dog’s barks)

5. Cultural transmission

Language is not genetically transmitted from generation to generation.

6. Discreteness

Language can be cut into segments and then be reorganized into meaningful sequences.

7. Interchangeability

All members of a speech community can be a sender and a receiver of messages.

8. Reflexivity

Human languages can be used to describe themselves.

The language used to talk about language is called ‘metalanguage’.

9. Adaptability

Language is an open system so that all social changes can be represented.

Functions of language

1. Informative function

2. Interpersonal function

3. Performative function

To change the social status of persons, as in marriage ceremonies, the sentencing of criminals, or the curing of enemies

To control reality, as on some magical or religious occasions

4. Emotive function

it is crucial in changing the emotional status of an audience for or against someone or something

“God, My, Damn it, What a sight, Wow, Ugh, Oh”

5. Phatic function

it refer to the social interaction 社会交往 of language

e.g. Greetings, farewell, slangs, jokes, jargons, ritualistic exchanges, switches to social and regional dialects

6. Recreational function

The use of language for the sheer joy of it
e.g. poetry writing or verbal dueling

7. Metalingual function

Our language can be used to talk about itself.

Linguistics may be defined as the scientific study of language’ (Lyons, 1968: 1).

Significant distinctions in Linguistics

1. Prescriptive规定式 vs. Descriptive 描写式

Prescriptive Descriptive
Don’t say X. People don’t say X.
prescribe how things ought to be describe how things are
In the 18th century, all the main European languages were studied prescriptively. modern linguistics
The nature of linguistics as a science determines its preoccupation with description instead of prescription.

2. Synchronic共时 vs. Diachronic历时

A synchronic description takes a fixed instant (usually, but not necessarily, the present) as its point of observation. Most grammars are of this kind.
Diachronic linguistics is the study of a language through the course of its history.

Speech sounds (1): Phonetics

What is Phonetics?

Phonetics studies how speech sounds are produced, transmitted, and perceived.

Articulatory phonetics 发音语音学 is the study of the production of speech sounds.

Acoustic phonetics 声学语音学 is the study of the physical properties of speech sounds.

Auditory phonetics 听觉语音学 is concerned with the perception of speech sounds.

Speech organs (articulators)

The sound-producing mechanism

Speech sounds are produced when the air is set in motion.

  1. The lungs produce the air stream.

  2. As the airstream flows out, it may be modified by the speech organs [at various points in various ways in the larynx, in oral or nasal cavities].

  3. A variety of sounds are thus produced.

    All the English and Chinese sounds are egressive 呼气的 sounds, cf. ingressive 吸气的 sounds–Arabic.

Phonetic transcription of speech sound (IPA)

English speech sounds

a. Consonants

Fundamental characteristics

Produced with a narrow or complete closure in the vocal tract（声道）. The air stream is either blocked momentarily or obstructed partially.

b. Vowels

Fundamental characteristics

Made with the vocal tract more open than it is for consonants. That is, there is hardly any obstruction of the air flow.

From word to text: Syntax

What is Syntax?

The study of the rules governing the ways different constituents are combined to form sentences in a language, or
The study of the interrelationships between elements in sentence structures.

Syntactic relations

1. Positional relation (Horizontal relation; Chain relation)

refers to the sequential arrangement of words in a language

2. Relation of substitutability (Vertical relation;

Choice relation)

type1: refers to classes or sets of words substitutable for each other grammatically in sentences with the same structure.

type2: groups of more than one word which may be jointly substitutable grammatically for a single word of a particular set.

3. Relation of Co-Occurrence

To form a sentence or a particular part of sentence, words of different sets of clauses may permit, or require, the occurrence of a word of another set or class

Grammatical construction and its constituents

Grammatical constructions

any syntactic construct which is assigned one or more conventional functions in a language, together with whatever is linguistically conventionalized about its construction to the meaning or use the construct contains.

语言中被指派了一个或多个规约功能的句法结构，以及对结构体的意义或用法的贡献。

On the level of syntax, we distinguish for any construction in a language its external and internal properties.

External syntax

the properties of the construction as a whole
clausal type or phrasal type

Internal syntax

a description of the construction’s ‘makeup’

‘subject’, ‘predicate’, ‘object’, or ‘determiner’

Immediate constituents

Constituent is a term used in structural sentence analysis for every linguistic unit, which a part of a larger linguistic unit. Several constituents together form a construction.

Endocentric and exocentric constructions

The syntactic constructions analyzed are of two main types.

根据句法结构的分布以及句法结构中成分之间的关系，我们所分析的句法结构可以分为两类。

1. Endocentric construction

one whose distribution is functionally equivalent to that of one or more of its constituents, i.e. a word or a group of words， which serves as a definable center or head.

向心结构的分布和它的一个或多个成分的分布在功能上相同，该成分是一个词或一组词，是整个结构的核心或中心。

名词短语、动词短语和形容词短语通常属于向心结构，因为各成分从属于短语中心词。

Coordination and Subordination

Endocentric construction fall into two main types, depending on the relation between constituents: Coordination and Subordination

  1. Coordination 并列结构

    is a common syntactic pattern in English and other languages formed by grouping together two or more categories of the same type with the help of a conjunction such as and, but and or.

    These two or more words or phrases or clauses have equivalent syntactic status.

    句法地位相同

    Each of the separate constituents can stand for the original construction functionally.

    在功能上可以代表原来的结构
    – Both of the conjoined elements: heads

    有两个中心成分

  2. Subordination 从属

    refers to the process or the result of linking linguistic unit so that they have different syntactic status, one being dependent upon the other.

    – words (constituents) which modify the Head

    从属指的是将语言单位连接以使各单位具备不同句法地位的过程或结果，其中一个单位依赖于另一个单位，且通常是该单位的一个成分。因而从属成分是修饰中心成分的词语。

2. Exocentric construction

refers to a group of syntactically related words where none of the words is functionally equivalent to the group as a whole

它指的是一组句法上相关的词中没有一个词在功能上与整个词组相同。也就是说，词组内没有“核心”或“中心词”。

离心结构通常包括：基本句（basic sentence），介词短语（prepositional phrase），谓语结构（predicate construction，动词+宾语）和连词结构（connective）（be + 补语 complement）。

e.g.
Alice smiled.
Tom hid behind the door.
Joseph kicked the ball.
John seemed angry.
